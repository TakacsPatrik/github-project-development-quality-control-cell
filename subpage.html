Overview - Problem Statement

I present the development of an optical, automated quality control cell capable of capturing high-resolution images of internally threaded components. The aim of the development was to implement a system suitable for visual data collection of components produced during manufacturing, thereby supporting the training of a future AI-based classification model.

Development process 1.

During the design of the custom-developed quality control cell, I first defined the system requirements, then selected the necessary hardware and software components accordingly. The cell’s frame was constructed from aluminum profiles, and the imaging system was composed of a monochrome industrial camera, telecentric optics, and a ring LED light source. The three-axis movement of the camera is handled by a precision lead screw system controlled by a motor controller. The positioning of the components is performed by a pneumatic unit, and the control is managed through a user-friendly graphical interface.

Development process 2.

Using image processing, I determined the outer tangent of the part by applying filtering and geometric rules. After noise reduction and edge detection, I extracted relevant contours and used shape-based criteria (e.g., perimeter and circularity) to accurately identify the outer edge. Finally, I unwrapped the part into a flat view for further analysis.

Development process 3.

After detecting the part’s outer edge with image processing, I automated centering. Since the ring can shift in the holder, I computed the contour’s geometric center from the camera image and compared it to a predefined reference. If the deviation exceeded the predefined tolerance, I repositioned the camera and restarted the inspection cycle. Stepper motors with 0.005 mm resolution provided the required 0.01 mm positioning accuracy.

Development process 4.

The system starts with operator login and program launch. The software then checks connections with the camera and motor controller; once initialized, status indicators turn green.

The camera performs a homing sequence using limit switches, then returns to a predefined position where the part is placed. After the operator inserts the part and clicks Capture Image, a pneumatic cylinder moves it under the camera for centering. The camera captures and unwraps the image for analysis.em>

Both the original and unwrapped images appear in the GUI, where the operator classifies the part (MR/NMR) and can add comments, such as the machine ID.em>

Development process 5.

During image processing, metadata is stored in a structured .txt file with a predefined format. This includes the operator ID (for traceability of subjective evaluations), part identifiers, counters, and inspection parameters, ensuring full process traceability.

Based on the recorded data, statistical analysis was performed, enabling process optimization. As a result, the cycle time was reduced from ~23 seconds to 12.99 seconds.em>

Key Achievements

  • Implementation of an automated visual quality inspection cell
  • Construction of a complex mechanical and automation system

  • Development of a user-friendly graphical interface
